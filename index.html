    ShelfSense AI

    Using powerful image recognition through your webcam, ShelfSense AI detects inventory items and analyzes stock levels in real-time using AI in real-time. Powered by Google's Teachable Machine.

    How Our AI Works

    Machine Learning Model

    Our system uses a convolutional neural network trained on thousands of images of fresh and spoiled produce. The model analyzes visual patterns including color distribution, texture variations, and surface defects.

    Transfer Learning

    We leverage transfer learning from pre-trained models like Teachable Machine, fine-tuning the final layers specifically for freshness detection. This allows for accurate results with less training data.

    Real-time Analysis

    The model runs directly in your browser using TensorFlow.js, processing each frame to provide immediate freshness assessments without sending images to external servers.

    Privacy Focused

    All image processing happens locally on your device. No images are stored or sent to any servers, ensuring your privacy and security.

    Scanning Guide

    Lighting Conditions

    Ensure good lighting on the produce. Natural daylight works best. Avoid shadows and harsh direct light that creates reflections.

    Distance & Angle

    Position the item 6-12 inches from the camera. Keep the camera parallel to the produce for best results.

    Framing

    Center the produce in the frame and make sure it takes up most of the view. Avoid including background objects.

    What to Avoid

    Avoid moving the camera during scanning. Don't scan through plastic bags or containers as they can cause reflections.
